SUYA RECIPES


Description

Suya is a beloved street food from Nigeria, made by skewering thin slices of meat—typically beef, chicken, or ram—and coating them in a bold, spicy peanut-based seasoning known as yaji. The meat is grilled over open charcoal, giving it a smoky, slightly charred flavor, while the spice mix adds heat, nuttiness, and a rich aroma. It’s usually served hot off the grill with fresh sliced onions, tomatoes, and cabbage, often with an extra sprinkle of spice on top. Simple but intensely flavorful, suya is known for its irresistible combination of heat, smoke, and savory depth.

Ingredient

  • 500g beef (sirloin or flank, thinly sliced)
  • 3 tbsp roasted peanuts (ground)
  • 1–2 tsp cayenne pepper (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 1 tsp ground ginger
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp onion powder
  • 1 bouillon cube (crushed)
  • Salt to taste
  • 2–3 tbsp vegetable oil

Steps

  1. Slice the meat thinly – the thinner, the better for that authentic texture.
  2. Coat with oil – helps the spices stick.
  3. Rub with spice mix – coat generously and let it marinate for at least 1 hour (overnight is best).
  4. Thread onto skewers (optional but traditional).
  5. Grill over charcoal (best) or use an oven/grill pan. Turn occasionally.
  6. Brush lightly with oil while grilling for extra juiciness.
  7. Sprinkle extra spice after grilling for that classic finish.
